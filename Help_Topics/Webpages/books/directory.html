Discover your next Favourite Book

Atomic Habits

James Clear

Self-Help 2018
View Details

Atomic Habits by James Clear is a powerful guide on how small, consistent changes can lead to remarkable improvements in your life. The book explains that habits are the compound interest of self-improvement—tiny changes, when done consistently, lead to significant results over time.

The Power of Now

Eckhart Tolle

Spiritual-Enlightenment 1997
View Details

The Power of Now by Eckhart Tolle is a spiritual guide that emphasizes the importance of living fully in the present moment. The book explores how our thoughts about the past and future often create unnecessary anxiety, stress, and unhappiness. Tolle teaches that true peace and fulfillment come from disconnecting from the constant chatter of the mind and becoming deeply aware of the “now.” Through simple yet profound insights, he encourages readers to shift their focus away from time-based thinking and into the present, where life truly unfolds. It’s a transformative read for anyone seeking inner peace and clarity.

The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People

Stephen R. Covey

1953 Effectiveness
View Details

The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People by Stephen R. Covey is a timeless self-help classic that offers a principle-centered approach to personal and professional effectiveness. The book outlines seven habits that help individuals achieve lasting success by aligning their actions with core values like integrity, responsibility, and proactive thinking. Covey's framework moves from personal mastery (habits like being proactive and beginning with the end in mind) to effective collaboration with others (like thinking win-win and synergizing). With its focus on character development and long-term growth, the book empowers readers to take control of their lives, improve relationships, and become more purpose-driven leaders.

Digital Minimalism

Cal Newport

Balancing 2019
View Details

Digital Minimalism by Cal Newport is a thought-provoking guide that encourages readers to take control of their digital lives by intentionally minimizing time spent on screens and social media. Newport argues that constant digital distractions are eroding our attention, productivity, and overall well-being. He introduces the philosophy of “digital minimalism,” which involves using technology only when it truly adds value to your life. Through practical advice, real-life examples, and a 30-day digital declutter challenge, the book helps readers reclaim their time, focus on meaningful activities, and create a healthier relationship with technology.

Think Like a Monk

Jay Shetty

Personal Growth 2020
View Details

Think Like a Monk by Jay Shetty offers practical wisdom from his time as a monk to help readers find peace, purpose, and clarity. The book teaches how to overcome negativity, improve focus, and live a more mindful, intentional life by mastering your thoughts and embracing inner values.

Ikigai

Hector Gracia & Francesc Miralles

Habitual Wellness 2016
View Details

Ikigai by Hector Gracia and Francesc Miralles explores the Japanese concept of Ikigai, which translates to “a reason for being.” The book uncovers the secrets of happiness and longevity by examining the lives of people in Okinawa, Japan, a region with the highest number of centenarians. It explores how to find purpose and meaning in life by identifying what you love, what you are good at, and what the world needs. This book offers insights into living a fulfilling life through mindfulness, balance, and a deep connection to personal values.
